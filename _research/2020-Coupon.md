### Abstract

<p class="text-justify">This paper examines the effect of child support payment coupons which were granted to subsidize households that are struggling due to COVID-19 on household spending. The results of difference-in-differences suggest that child support payment coupons increased household spending by a week average of 22,019 KRW over 10 weeks, which is 8.7% of the treatment group’s average consumption for the period of analysis. Child support payment coupons have positive impacts on supporting both children and small-scale business-owners. The effect of child support payment coupons on household spending is shown to be significantly larger for low income households, and the spending of top 20% households were not affected by the coupons. We find that coupon redemption substitutes some of the household spending if there were no support by the government. The size of the net effect (week average of 22,019 KRW over 10 weeks) is determined by the difference from coupon redemption (128,965 KRW) and the size of the substitution effect (106,947 KRW). These results imply that the net effect of the coupons is estimated to be 17% of coupon redemption for 10 weeks. We further discuss implications of our findings on the effects and design of government subsidies.</p> 

<b>Key words:</b> child support coupons, household spending, card data
